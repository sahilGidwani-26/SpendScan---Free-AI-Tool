# GTM — Go-To-Market Plan

## Exact target user

**Primary:** Engineering manager or CTO at a 10-50 person seed/Series A startup. They handle vendor procurement decisions, feel the pain of SaaS sprawl, and get the monthly AI billing emails. They're technical enough to evaluate tools but too busy to audit spend proactively.

**Secondary:** Indie hacker or solo founder running 1-3 paid AI subscriptions, trying to control burn rate.

Not: enterprise procurement (too many stakeholders), consumer users (don't pay for AI), or companies with dedicated FinOps (they already have this).

## What they Google/scroll before they'd want this tool

- "cursor vs github copilot cost comparison 2026"
- "claude pro vs team plan which is better"
- "AI tools too expensive startup budget"
- "is chatgpt team worth it"
- Scrolling through Hacker News "Ask HN: how much are you paying for AI tools per developer?"
- Seeing a tweet about "AI bill this month 💀"

## Where they hang out online

- **r/SaaS**, **r/startups**, **r/ExperiencedDevs** — active engineering/ops discussion
- **Hacker News** — Show HN and Ask HN posts get immediate feedback from the exact demographic
- **Indie Hackers** — "Roast my stack" threads, monthly expense reports
- **Slack: Rands Leadership, Software Lead Weekly** — engineering manager communities
- **X (Twitter) lists:** "YC founders", "Build in public", "Indie hackers" curated lists
- **Discord: Anthropic developer, OpenAI developer servers** — people actively using these APIs

## First 100 users in 30 days — $0 budget

**Week 1 — Reddit + community seeding**
- Post to r/SaaS: "I built a free tool that audits your AI tool spend — found $400/mo in waste at my startup." Personal story, link in comments after discussion.
- Post to r/ExperiencedDevs: Same framing but more technical — include the audit engine logic reasoning.
- Comment in active threads on r/startups that ask about AI tool recommendations.

**Week 2 — Hacker News**
- "Show HN: SpendScan — free AI spend audit for startups" post. 
- Timing: Tuesday or Wednesday 8-9am ET (peak HN upvote window).
- Have 3 friends ready to comment with genuine questions to kickstart discussion.

**Week 3 — Twitter/X**
- Thread: "We audited 50 startup AI stacks. Here's what we found:" → aggregate anonymized data from first users → share surprising findings (e.g., "74% of startups with Cursor Business would save money on Pro").
- Target retweets from YC founders/operators who share SaaS efficiency content.

**Week 4 — Direct outreach**
- Cold DM 20 indie hackers/founders on X who have publicly shared their monthly expenses (these people love transparency tools).
- Message: "Hey, I built this because I noticed I was overpaying for Claude — would love your take."

**Unfair distribution channel:** Credex already has relationships with companies buying AI credits — they can email that list directly. A startup that just bought discounted Cursor credits from Credex is the perfect person to audit their full stack next. Internal list of existing customers is the unfair channel no cold outreach campaign can replicate.

## Week-1 traction if this works

- 200+ audits completed (tracking: MongoDB audit doc count)
- 40+ email captures (20% conversion from audit → lead — B2B benchmarks suggest 15-25%)
- 3-5 Credex consultation bookings from high-savings users
- ≥1 tweet that gets >50 RT from a founder with 5k+ followers
- Show HN: top 30 for the day = ~500-1,000 additional visitors

## What scores well (being honest)

The unfair channel and the specific Hacker News timing/seeding approach are real tactics. The thread idea with aggregated data is strong — it's a reason to tweet that isn't just "look at my tool." The weakest part of this GTM is cold DMs (low response rate) but they're zero-cost and occasionally land.
