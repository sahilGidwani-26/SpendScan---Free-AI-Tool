# LANDING_COPY

## Headline

**Chosen:** "Are you overpaying for AI tools?"

**Why it works:**
- It's a question, not a claim — questions create cognitive engagement, the reader answers it internally ("...maybe?")
- Addresses a real anxiety without being alarmist
- "Overpaying" is the exact word the target user uses internally ("I feel like we're overpaying for this")

**Alternatives tested (copy testing / gut check):**

| Option | Why rejected |
|--------|-------------|
| "Stop wasting money on AI tools" | Too aggressive. Implies they're dumb. |
| "AI tool cost audit" | Descriptive but zero emotion. Doesn't pull. |
| "Find out what your AI stack really costs" | Vague — they already know what it costs, they want to know if it's *too much* |
| "The free AI spend calculator for startups" | Too product-focused, not benefit-focused |

---

## Subheadline

**Chosen:** "Most startups overspend $200–800/month on AI subscriptions — wrong plan, too many seats, or duplicate tools. Find out in 60 seconds."

**Why it works:**
- Specific dollar range ($200–800) — credible, not vague "thousands"
- Names the three exact failure modes (wrong plan / too many seats / duplicate tools) — reader sees their situation in at least one
- "60 seconds" — removes the "is this worth my time?" objection immediately

---

## CTA

**Primary:** "Run free audit →"

**Why:** "Free" removes the pay-friction objection. "Run" is more active than "Start" or "Try" — it implies you're running a process, something is about to happen. Arrow signals forward motion.

**Secondary (under CTA):** "No email required to see results"

**Why:** Removes the biggest objection to clicking ("I'll have to give my email and get spammed"). This one line likely lifts CTR by 20-30% based on standard landing page patterns.

---

## Social proof stats

Chosen stats:
- "Average monthly overspend: $340"
- "Tools commonly over-licensed: 3 of 5"  
- "Minutes to complete audit: < 2"

**Why these three:**
- First stat establishes the problem is real and significant
- Second makes it concrete — not abstract "AI tools" but a ratio that implies most stacks have issues
- Third removes the time objection

**Note:** These are estimates at launch. After 50+ real audits, replace with real data from MongoDB analytics.

---

## "How it works" section

Used 3 steps numbered 01/02/03 (not 1/2/3 — the leading zero creates visual weight and a more editorial feel).

Each step is problem-aware:
- "Enter your AI tools" (acknowledges they have multiple)
- "Get instant analysis" (emphasizes speed)
- "See your savings" (outcome-first, not "get recommendations")

---

## Final CTA section (dark background)

**Headline:** "Your AI spend has a leak."

**Why:** "Leak" is a concrete metaphor. Water leaks are wasteful, invisible, and fixable. It implies ongoing loss, not a one-time mistake. Creates urgency without being hypey.

**Sub:** "Run the audit. It's free, takes 2 minutes, and you'll know exactly where the money's going."

**Why "where the money's going":** This frames the audit as gaining clarity, not just finding problems. People want to feel informed, not blamed.
